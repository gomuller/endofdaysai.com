Session 09: Writing the First Outreach Message

Once you have a clear summary, the next step is outreach. A project does not move forward only because the idea is good. It moves forward when the right people are invited into the work.

Outreach does not need to be complicated. In fact, the best first message is usually simple, human, and direct. It should explain who you are, what you are working on, why it matters, and what kind of response or next step you are hoping for.

AI can help you draft that first message. It can help you adjust the tone, shorten the language, make the ask clearer, and create different versions for donors, partners, volunteers, or community leaders.

But the message still needs to sound like you. People respond to sincerity more than polish. AI can help with structure, but your honesty and relationship matter most.

Discussion Points

  • Who is one person or organization you could contact first?
  • What do you want them to understand?
  • What is the specific next step you are asking for?
  • How can your message sound clear without sounding transactional?

Prompt to Try

Help me write a short, sincere outreach message about my project. The message should explain what I am working on, why it matters, who it helps, and invite the person to a next conversation. Give me three versions: warm, professional, and brief.
