Session 03: The Story Funders Understand

A clear project and a defined audience are essential—but they are not enough. People do not respond only to information. They respond to meaning.

This is where story comes in. Not storytelling as exaggeration or performance, but as a way of connecting purpose, people, and change into something others can understand and feel.

A strong project story answers a few key questions: What is happening? Why does it matter? Who is affected? What changes if this project succeeds?

Without this, a project can feel technical, abstract, or distant. With it, the project becomes human. It becomes something others can step into and support.

AI can help you shape this story. It can organize your ideas, suggest structure, simplify language, and help you connect the different parts into a clear narrative. But it still depends on you to provide the truth behind the story.

The goal is not to impress—it is to help someone understand enough to care.

Discussion Points

  • What is the story behind your project?
  • Why does this work matter right now?
  • What changes if your project succeeds?
  • What part of your story feels strongest? What feels unclear?

Prompt to Try

Help me turn my project into a clear and compelling story. Organize it so someone unfamiliar can understand what is happening, why it matters, who is affected, and what change this project creates.
