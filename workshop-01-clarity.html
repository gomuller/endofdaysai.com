Session 01: Clarity Before Funding

Most projects do not struggle first because they lack money. They struggle because they lack clarity. A funder, donor, partner, or supporter cannot easily help a project they do not understand.

Clarity is the first form of preparation. Before asking anyone for support, we need to be able to explain what the project is, who it serves, why it matters, what problem it addresses, and what change it hopes to create. When those pieces are unclear, even a worthy project can sound vague, scattered, or unfinished.

This is one of the places where AI can become a powerful ally. AI can help us slow down and organize our thoughts. It can ask clarifying questions, identify missing details, simplify complicated language, and help us move from a rough idea to a clear project description.

But AI does not create the mission. The mission comes from us. AI helps us express it. It can sharpen the language, but it cannot replace the heart behind the work.

In funding, clarity is not decoration. It is an act of respect. It respects the people being served, the people being asked to help, and the work itself.

Discussion Points

  • Why do good projects sometimes fail to attract support?
  • What makes a project easy or hard to explain?
  • What part of your project is currently clearest?
  • What part still feels vague or unfinished?

Prompt to Try

Help me clearly describe my project in simple language. Ask me one question at a time until you understand the purpose, audience, problem, solution, and desired impact.
