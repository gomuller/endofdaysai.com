Session 08: Build Your One-Page Funding Summary

This is where everything comes together. You now have clarity, a defined audience, a meaningful story, and an understanding of potential supporters. The next step is to bring it into one clear, usable form.

A one-page funding summary is one of the most powerful tools you can create. It gives you something you can share, send, explain, and build from. It does not need to be perfect—it needs to be clear.

Think of this as your “working version.” You will refine it over time, but this first version gives your project a starting point in the real world.

What to Include

  • Project name and simple description
  • Who it serves
  • The problem or need
  • The solution or approach
  • The impact if successful
  • Basic idea of funding needed (if known)

AI can help you assemble this quickly. The goal is not perfection—the goal is clarity and momentum.

Discussion Points

  • What is easiest for you to write in your summary?
  • What part feels hardest or least clear?
  • What would make your summary easier for someone else to understand?
  • What would make someone more likely to support this?

Prompt to Try

Help me create a clear one-page funding summary for my project. Organize it into sections: project overview, audience, problem, solution, impact, and funding need. Ask me questions if information is missing, and keep the language simple and clear.
