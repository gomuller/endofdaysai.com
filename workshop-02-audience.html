Session 02: Who Does This Help?

Every meaningful project serves someone. But many projects describe their audience too broadly: “the community,” “people in need,” or “everyone.” While those may be true, they are not clear enough to inspire action.

Funders and supporters respond to specificity. They want to understand who is being helped, what their situation is, what they are facing, and what changes if the project succeeds.

When the people being served become clear, the project becomes real. It moves from an idea to a human story.

This is another place where AI can help. It can help you describe your audience more clearly, identify different groups within that audience, and articulate their needs, challenges, and desired outcomes.

But again, AI does not know your people—you do. AI helps you describe them more precisely so others can understand and respond.

Discussion Points

  • Who specifically does your project help?
  • What is their current situation?
  • What challenges are they facing?
  • What changes if your project succeeds?

Prompt to Try

Help me clearly define the people my project serves. Ask me questions to identify their situation, needs, challenges, and what success would look like for them.
