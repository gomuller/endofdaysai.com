Session 02: Alignment Before Strategy

Most workshops—especially those focused on fundraising, business, or strategy— begin in the same place: tactics. Tools, frameworks, scripts, optimization. In a modern setting, that often includes AI.

And while those things matter, they often skip something deeper—something that, for many people, sits underneath everything else.

Call it what you want: God, a higher power, the Universe, intuition, or simply being “in flow.” Different traditions use different language, and some avoid the topic entirely. In fact, most secular environments leave it out because it feels too personal, too abstract, or too controversial.

But if we are being honest, many of the most meaningful outcomes in life—funding, opportunities, connections—do not come through strategy alone. They often emerge through alignment. Through a sense that you are moving with something, not just pushing against resistance.

When people describe their biggest breakthroughs, they rarely talk only about tactics. They talk about timing. About meeting the right person unexpectedly. About things “falling into place” in ways they could not have fully engineered.

This does not replace strategy—it reframes it. Instead of starting with tools, we start with intention, clarity, and alignment. Then we use tools like AI to amplify that direction.

So the question is not just “How do I raise money?” It is also: What am I aligned with? Why does this matter? And am I moving from force—or from flow?

Discussion Points

  • Why do you think most workshops avoid discussing alignment or higher purpose?
  • What language feels most natural to you: God, flow, intuition, something else—or none?
  • Have you experienced moments where outcomes felt aligned rather than forced?
  • How might alignment influence the way you approach fundraising?
  • Can strategy and spirituality coexist, or do they feel at odds?
  • How might AI be used after alignment is established, rather than as the starting point?

Prompt to Try

Help me reflect on my project from a deeper perspective. Ask me questions that help clarify what I feel called to do, why it matters, and what alignment would look like before focusing on strategy or execution.
