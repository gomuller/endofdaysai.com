Minute 7

Prompt: Which of those 5 would you recommend that we use as an example for the grant funding workshop tomorrow — one that is straightforward, and more simple maybe?

Best Choice: Utah CCJJ Justice Grants (JAG)

Utah Commission on Criminal and Juvenile Justice

Why this is your best workshop example

  • Simple + practical: Designed for local nonprofits, straightforward application language, not buried in federal complexity.
  • Direct alignment with Adullam: Criminal justice support, reentry services, advocacy, and system improvement.
  • Teachable structure: Problem statement, program design, outcomes, and budget without getting lost in federal compliance language.
  • Realistic win potential: It feels possible, not abstract.

Backup Option: Utah Bar Foundation (IOLTA)

Also good, but slightly more niche because it requires a stronger legal services framing and is less broad than CCJJ.

Not Ideal for a Basic Workshop Example

  • Bureau of Justice Assistance grants — heavy federal requirements
  • Public Welfare Foundation — relationship-based, abstract
  • The Just Trust — strategic and network-driven

These are Phase 2 targets, not teaching tools.

Workshop Framing

“We’re going to use a real, winnable grant — Utah CCJJ — as our example. If you can write this one clearly, you can write almost any grant.”

Sample Grant Application

Adullam Justice Project

Project Title

Community-Based Justice Advocacy & Reentry Support Program

1. Problem Statement

Many individuals in Utah who are involved in the criminal justice system—particularly those who are wrongly accused, under-resourced, or reentering society after incarceration— lack access to coordinated support.

These individuals often face:

  • Limited legal guidance prior to trial
  • Disconnection from advocacy resources
  • Barriers to successful reentry, including housing, employment, and support systems

As a result:

  • Cases may be poorly navigated
  • Families are destabilized
  • Recidivism risk increases

There is a clear need for community-based support systems that bridge gaps between individuals, legal resources, and reintegration pathways.

2. Program Description

Adullam Justice Project proposes a Community-Based Justice Advocacy & Reentry Support Program that provides structured support to justice-involved individuals and their families.

The program will:

  • Provide Justice Advocacy Support
    Assist individuals pre-trial with understanding their legal situation, connecting to appropriate legal resources, and preparing for court processes.
  • Coordinate Resource Navigation
    Connect participants with legal professionals, social services, and community support organizations.
  • Support Reentry and Reintegration
    Provide guidance and referrals for employment readiness, housing resources, and community reintegration.
  • Engage Volunteer Advocates
    Train community members to serve as “Citizen Advocates” and support partners for individuals and families.

3. Target Population

The program will serve:

  • Justice-involved individuals in Utah, pre-trial and post-incarceration
  • Families impacted by incarceration
  • Individuals with limited financial or legal resources

Priority will be given to:

  • First-time offenders
  • Individuals at high risk of recidivism
  • Those lacking access to legal support

4. Goals and Outcomes

  • Goal 1: Improve Access to Justice Support
    75 individuals receive advocacy support annually; 80% connected to appropriate legal or support resources.
  • Goal 2: Strengthen Reentry Outcomes
    60 individuals receive reentry support; 65% demonstrate progress toward employment or stability.
  • Goal 3: Build Community Engagement
    20 volunteer advocates trained; increased community participation in justice support efforts.

5. Implementation Plan

The program will be implemented over a 12-month period:

  • Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Setup
    Finalize program structure, recruit and train volunteers, establish partnerships.
  • Phase 2 (Months 4–10): Service Delivery
    Begin intake and case support, provide advocacy and referrals, track participant progress.
  • Phase 3 (Months 11–12): Evaluation
    Assess outcomes, gather participant feedback, prepare reports.

6. Budget Overview

Category Amount
Program Coordination$25,000
Volunteer Training$5,000
Participant Support (transport, resources)$7,500
Administrative Costs$5,000
Total Requested$42,500

7. Organizational Capacity

Adullam Justice Project is a grassroots nonprofit dedicated to justice advocacy, support for justice-involved individuals, and community-based restoration.

The organization operates through:

  • Volunteer advocates
  • Community partnerships
  • A growing network of legal and social resources

Adullam’s flexible, relationship-based model allows it to:

  • Respond quickly to individual needs
  • Connect people to the right resources
  • Support both individuals and families

8. Evaluation

Program success will be measured through:

  • Number of individuals served
  • Resource connections made
  • Participant progress toward stability
  • Volunteer engagement levels

Data will be tracked through:

  • Intake forms
  • Case notes
  • Follow-up assessments

How to Use This in the Workshop

Walk through the example like this:

  1. Problem → What’s broken?
  2. Program → What are we doing about it?
  3. People → Who are we helping?
  4. Outcomes → What changes?
  5. Budget → What does it cost?

Pro Tip for Delivery

Pause after each section and ask:

“Does this reflect Adullam’s real work?”

That turns this from a sample into a live co-creation of the real grant.
