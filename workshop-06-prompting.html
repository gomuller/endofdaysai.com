Session 06: How to Prompt Like a Project Leader

AI becomes more useful when you stop treating it like a search box and start treating it like a thinking partner. A weak prompt asks for an answer. A strong prompt gives context, defines the goal, and invites better questions.

Project leaders do not need perfect wording. They need a clear process. Tell AI what you are trying to accomplish, who the project serves, what kind of help you need, and what format would be useful.

The best prompts often ask AI to slow down. Instead of saying, “Write me a grant,” ask it to interview you first. Ask it to identify missing information. Ask it to challenge vague language. Ask it to help you think before it helps you write.

This changes the relationship. AI is no longer just producing text. It is helping you clarify judgment, prepare action, and communicate with greater confidence.

Discussion Points

  • What makes a prompt too vague?
  • How can AI help you think before it writes?
  • What context should you always give AI about your project?
  • Why is it useful to ask AI to ask you questions first?

Prompt to Try

Act as a thoughtful project funding coach. Interview me one question at a time about my project. Help me clarify the mission, audience, need, impact, budget, timeline, and possible supporters before drafting any materials.
