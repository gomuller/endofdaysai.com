Session 07: What AI Cannot Replace

AI is powerful—but it is not complete. It can assist thinking, organize ideas, and generate language. But there are parts of meaningful work it cannot replace.

It cannot build trust. It cannot form real relationships. It cannot carry moral responsibility. It cannot serve in the place of lived experience, accountability, or integrity.

This matters especially in funding. People do not support projects only because they are well-written. They support projects because they trust the people behind them. They believe in their purpose. They see consistency between words and actions.

AI can help you communicate—but it cannot make you credible. It can help you organize—but it cannot make you accountable. It can help you respond—but it cannot choose your intent.

The strongest use of AI is not to replace these things, but to support them. When used with integrity, it can help good people do good work more clearly. When used without it, it can amplify confusion or misrepresentation.

Discussion Points

  • What parts of your project depend on trust?
  • What cannot be outsourced to AI?
  • How do integrity and clarity work together?
  • What risks come from relying too heavily on AI?

Prompt to Try

Help me review my project materials for clarity and honesty. Identify anything that sounds vague, exaggerated, or unclear, and suggest ways to improve it while staying truthful and grounded.
